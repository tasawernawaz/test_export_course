Welcome to 2.854.1x Introduction to Manufacturing Systems Engineering. This is the second module in our new Principles of Manufacturing MicroMasters program, and while our focus here is on the topic of randomness and variation in manufacturing settings, we urge you to look at the complete set of modules in the MicroMasters, which all revolve around the theme of flow (of material and information) and variation at various levels of manufacturing. 

If you're new to edX we highly encourage you to view DemoX to learn about the edX learning experience.

There will be one timed exam in this course - be sure to block out your schedule accordingly. The final exam will be released on October 23rd and must be completed prior to October 30th. This date, as well as all due dates for the weekly Graded Problem Sets are fixed and cannot be changed or modified for any individuals, please plan accordingly.

Pre-requisite:
6.041.1x or equivalent. Knowledge and comfortability with undergraduate-level calculus, probability and statistics.

MicroMaster's Section
In each week, there will be a MicroMaster's section that is only accessible to verified students. In it you will find additional practice problems, selected theses/papers from MIT students as well as exclusive access to Webinars and other events hosted by the staff. The first Webinar will be available to all students including audit students and will be offered in the second week of the course, the remaining will be determined at a later date.

What to Expect from 2.854.1x:
This course will follow a weekly program for a total of 7 weeks of content. Each week, you will receive new lecture videos, finger exercises and a homework assignment (except for week 1) as we build toward the final exam. The best way you can prepare for the course now is to ensure that you have adequate time scheduled in the coming weeks to complete the course.

What You'll Learn:

  • Probability Basics
  • Stochastic Processes
  • Queuing Theory
  • Inventory Management
  • Toyota Production System
  • Single Part Type Manufacturing Lines
